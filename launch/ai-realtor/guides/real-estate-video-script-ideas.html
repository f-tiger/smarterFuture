7 Real Estate Video Script Ideas (With Templates)

ListingLift · Practical AI for real-estate agents

Video is the fastest way to build trust at scale, but "just be authentic" isn't a plan. A short script is. Every good real estate video follows the same shape: a hook in the first three seconds, a quick payoff, and one clear next step. Below are seven scripts you can shoot today — each with a fill-in template. Keep them 20–60 seconds.

1. The 60-second listing tour

Hook: "You will not believe what [$price] gets you in [Neighborhood]." Body: walk through three standout features, one shot each — "Start here in the [kitchen with new quartz counters]... then this [light-filled living room]... and outside, [a yard built for summer]." CTA: "Comment 'TOUR' and I'll send the full gallery."

2. The neighborhood guide

Hook: "Thinking about moving to [Neighborhood]? Here's what locals actually love." Body: three quick spots — "[Coffee shop] for mornings, [park/trail] for weekends, and [restaurant] you'll be telling friends about." CTA: "DM me 'MOVE' if you want the full neighborhood breakdown."

3. The 60-second market update

Hook: "Here's what's actually happening in the [City] market this month." Body: one plain-English trend and what it means — "Homes in [Neighborhood] are [sitting a little longer / moving fast], which means [buyers have more room to negotiate / sellers should price sharp]." CTA: "Want this for your street? DM me 'UPDATE.'"

4. The "biggest mistake" tip

Hook: "The #1 mistake I see [first-time buyers] make..." Body: name the mistake, then the fix in one line — "They [skip getting pre-approved] and fall for a home they can't move on. Do [this] first." CTA: "Follow for one home-buying tip a week."

5. The myth vs. fact

Hook: "Myth: [you need 20% down to buy a home]." Body: "Fact: [many buyers put down far less]. Here's what that actually looks like for someone buying in [City]." CTA: "Got a question? Drop it below and I'll answer it in a video."

6. The day-in-the-life

Hook: "Come spend a day with a [City] real estate agent." Body: quick cuts — "8am [previewing a new listing], noon [a closing], 3pm [showing this gorgeous [Neighborhood] home]." CTA: "This could be your home next — DM me to start."

7. The client win (with permission)

Hook: "These two thought they couldn't buy this year. Watch this." Body: tell the short arc — "They came to me [worried about their down payment]. We [made a plan], and [last week they got the keys]." CTA: "If you've been putting it off, let's talk — DM 'START.'"

Tip: Nail the first three seconds or nothing else matters. Open on motion or a bold claim — "[$price] in [Neighborhood]?!" — not a slow intro or a logo. Viewers decide to keep watching almost instantly, so put your hook first and save the "hi, I'm [Name]" for the middle, if at all.

How to actually get these made

  • Write the hook and CTA first. The middle is easy once those two anchors exist.
  • Film verticals in good light. A window and a clean background beat any fancy gear.
  • Add captions. Most people watch on mute — on-screen text keeps them watching.
  • Keep it accurate and Fair Housing safe. Don't promise features the home doesn't have, and describe the property, not who "should" live there.
  • Batch it. Shoot three or four in one outfit, one afternoon. Future-you will be grateful.
