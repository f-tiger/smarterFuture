Seller Objection Scripts for the Listing Appointment

ListingLift · Practical AI for real-estate agents

Buyers hesitate; sellers push back. At the listing table you'll hear the same handful of objections in nearly every appointment, and the agents who win the listing aren't the ones who "overcome" them — they're the ones who answer calmly, with proof, and without getting defensive. Below are the five you'll hear most, with a response pattern for each. Adapt the wording to your voice; the structure is what matters.

1. "Your commission is too high / can you cut it?"

Don't defend the number or cave immediately. Do reframe from cost to net.

Script: "That's a fair question — let's look at it in terms of what you walk away with, not just the rate. My job is to net you more than you'd get otherwise, even after the fee. Here's how my marketing and pricing plan does that…" Then show the plan. If you discount reflexively the moment you're asked, you've told them your first number wasn't real.

2. "I think my home is worth more than that"

Don't argue with their number. Do let the comps do the talking.

Script: "I hear you — and I'd love for it to be. Let's walk through the three most recent sales that a buyer's agent and appraiser will actually compare yours to, and you tell me where you see it fitting." This moves it from your opinion vs. theirs to the market's evidence. Bring a genuinely defensible range — see how to do a CMA and framing the price narrative.

3. "Why should I list with you instead of [other agent]?"

Don't bad-mouth the competition. Do be specific about your plan.

Script: "Great question — you should interview a few of us. What I'd point to is the specific plan for your home: here's the pricing strategy, here's the first-week marketing, here's how I'll communicate with you. Compare that plan side by side with anyone else's." A concrete plan beats generic "I work hard" every time. See listing presentation tips.

4. "I'm just going to sell to an iBuyer / for cash"

Don't dismiss it. Do quantify the trade-off honestly.

Script: "That's a real option, and for some sellers the speed and certainty are worth it. Let's compare apples to apples: here's the likely net from that offer, and here's the likely net on the open market with my plan, minus the convenience. If the gap is small and you value certainty, take it — I'll tell you straight." Being the honest advisor often wins the listing the pushy agent loses.

5. "Let me think about it / talk to my spouse"

Don't pressure. Do surface the real hesitation.

Script: "Of course — this is a big decision. So I can be helpful, what's the one thing you'd most want to feel good about before moving forward? Price, timing, the plan?" This turns a stall into a specific concern you can actually address, instead of a vague no.

Tip: Practice these out loud before the appointment, not during it. You can even draft and rehearse your own versions with AI — paste your market's specifics and ask for three response variations in your voice. See saved AI prompts.

The mindset underneath all five

Objections aren't attacks — they're the seller telling you exactly what they need reassurance on. Answer the concern, not the ego, and lean on evidence over persuasion. The agent who stays calm and specific is the one who leaves with a signed agreement. Next step after you win it: the listing launch checklist.
