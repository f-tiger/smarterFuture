How to Do a CMA That Wins Listings (Step by Step)

ListingLift · Practical AI for real-estate agents

A CMA has two jobs: get the price right, and get the seller to believe the price. Most agents do the spreadsheet and skip the story — then wonder why the seller "went with the agent who said a higher number." Here's the full process, including the part that wins the listing.

Step 1: Pick comps a seller can't argue with

  • Sold, not listed. Active listings are opinions; closed sales are facts. Use solds from the last 90 days when you can, 180 max.
  • Tight radius, same market. Same neighborhood or school zone beats "one mile as the crow flies" across a highway.
  • Like for like. Within ~20% of the square footage, same era of construction, similar lot. Three to five strong comps beat ten loose ones.

Step 2: Adjust like an appraiser (lite)

Work from each comp's sold price and adjust toward the subject home. Keep a simple, consistent sheet: beds/baths, garage, condition/updates, lot, and one line for anything unusual. Two rules keep you honest:

  • Adjust the comp, not your hopes: if the comp has a renovated kitchen and the subject doesn't, the comp's price comes down for comparison.
  • If total adjustments pass ~15% of the price, it's not a comp. Drop it.

Step 3: Land on a range, not a number

Present "homes like yours are selling between [low] and [high]; where we list inside that range decides your traffic" — then show what each end buys: the low end brings multiple offers and speed, the top end needs perfect condition and patience. Now the seller is choosing a strategy, not fighting your number.

Step 4: Tell the price story

This is the part that wins listings. Walk the comps in person, with photos: "This one on [Street] got [Price] — but look at the kitchen. This one matches your finishes and got [Price] in nine days." You're not asserting a price; you're letting the market speak while you narrate. The price narrative guide shows how to write this story, and AI can draft your walkthrough script from the raw comps in minutes.

Step 5: Handle "but Zillow says…"

  • "Good instinct to check it. An estimate is a formula that has never seen your kitchen, your lot, or your street noise. It's often within a few percent — and a few percent on your home is [dollar amount]. That's what the comp walk we just did is for."
Stay in your lane: a CMA is a broker price opinion, not an appraisal. Say so on the document, follow your state's rules on BPO language, and never inflate the number to win the signature — the market re-prices overpriced listings publicly, on your brand. Package it

One page of comps, one range with the strategy trade-off, one paragraph of story. Bring it inside a tight listing presentation, and let our free AI tools turn your comp notes into clean seller-facing language.
