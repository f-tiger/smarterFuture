Real Estate Instagram Bio Ideas (With 10 Examples)

ListingLift · Practical AI for real-estate agents

Your Instagram bio is 150 characters of prime real estate, and most agents waste it on a job title. People who land on your profile already know you sell homes. What they want to know in two seconds is: who do you help, where, and what should they do next? This guide gives you a repeatable formula and ten bios you can adapt today.

The four-line bio formula

Instagram bios read best as short, stacked lines rather than one run-on sentence. Use this structure:

  • Line 1 — Who + where: your role and market, stated plainly. "Realtor in [City], [State]."
  • Line 2 — Who you help or what you're known for: first-time buyers, downsizers, relocations, a specific neighborhood.
  • Line 3 — A reason to trust or follow: a credential, a brokerage, years in the area, or the value you post (market updates, neighborhood tours).
  • Line 4 — One clear call to action: "DM 'TOUR' to see homes" and a single link.

Keep your name field (separate from the bio) keyword-friendly: "Jordan Lee | [City] Realtor" helps you surface in search. Save the bio text for personality and the CTA.

10 examples to edit

Swap the bracketed [merge fields] for your details. Each stays under the character limit and ends with one action.

  • New agent: "🏡 Helping first-time buyers in [City] | Honest answers, zero pressure | [Brokerage] | DM 'START' for a free buyer guide 👇"
  • Local expert: "📍 Your [Neighborhood] specialist | Lived & sold here [X] years | Weekly market updates | DM 'HOME' to chat 👇"
  • Luxury: "Curating exceptional [City] homes | By appointment | [Brokerage] | Private listings → link below"
  • Relocation: "✈️ Moving to [City]? I make it easy | Out-of-state buyers welcome | Free relocation guide 👇"
  • Seller-focused: "Thinking of selling in [City]? | I'll show you what your home is worth | DM 'VALUE' for a free estimate 👇"
  • Team: "The [Team Name] @ [Brokerage] | Buying & selling across [Metro Area] | 5★ from [X]+ families | Book a call below"
  • Investor-friendly: "Realtor + investor in [City] | Cash flow, fixers & first homes | Run the numbers with me → DM 'DEAL'"
  • Lifestyle: "Selling [City] homes & the lifestyle that comes with them | Coffee shops, schools, trails | Tours → link below"
  • Bilingual: "Realtor in [City] 🇺🇸🇲🇽 | Te ayudo a comprar y vender | Hablamos español | DM 'HOLA' 👇"
  • Just-licensed & building trust: "New to real estate, not to [City] | [X] years living here | Ask me anything about buying or selling | DM open 👇"
Tip: Pick a single DM keyword (TOUR, VALUE, START) and use it everywhere — bio, captions, stories. When people type it, you get a clean lead and an easy first reply. One keyword beats five vague CTAs.

Small details that make a difference

  • One link, well chosen. Point it to your home search, a free guide, or a link-in-bio page — not your generic brokerage homepage.
  • Emojis as bullets, not decoration. A 📍 or 🏡 at the start of a line adds structure and scannability. Two or three is plenty.
  • Skip Fair Housing trouble. Describe what you do, not who "should" live somewhere. "Helping first-time buyers" is fine; targeting by family status, religion, or similar is not.
  • Match your name field to search. "[Name] | [City] Realtor" helps the right locals find you.
