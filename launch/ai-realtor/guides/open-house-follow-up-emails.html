Open House Follow-Up Emails (Templates That Get Replies)

ListingLift · Practical AI for real-estate agents

The average open house visitor tours three homes that weekend and forgets every agent they met by Monday. The agent who follows up first, with something specific, is the one who exists. Here's a complete follow-up sequence — copy it, fill the [merge fields], send the first one before dinner.

Same evening: the thank-you that stands out

Subject: Great meeting you at [Address] today

"Hi [First name], thanks for coming through [Address] this afternoon — I hope the [feature they commented on] lived up to the photos. You mentioned you're [their situation: just starting to look / hoping to move by summer]. Two quick things: the seller's disclosure and floor plan are attached in case it stays on your list, and if it's not quite right, tell me the one thing you'd change — that usually tells me exactly which other homes to send you. Either way, enjoy the rest of the weekend!"

The magic is [feature they commented on]. Take notes during the open house — one detail per visitor — and every email becomes impossible to mistake for a blast.

Day 2: the value follow-up

Subject: 3 homes like [Address] (one just listed)

"Hi [First name] — based on what you said Saturday, here are three homes worth a look: [Listing 1 with one-line reason], [Listing 2], [Listing 3]. The second one just hit the market and homes on that street move fast. Want me to set up a showing for any of them this week?"

Day 5–7: the soft close

Subject: Should I keep an eye out for you?

"Hi [First name], last note from me unless you'd like more: I set up searches for buyers at no cost — you get new [Neighborhood] listings the hour they go live, before the portals surface them. Want me to set one up with your criteria? If the timing's not right, no worries at all — just say the word and I'll leave you be."

That last sentence matters. An easy exit gets you honest answers, and honest answers are how you spend time on real buyers.

Don't forget the neighbors — they're seller leads

Neighbors who "just wanted a peek" are next year's listings. Send them a different email:

"Hi [First name], nice meeting you at the open house on [Street]! Since you're just up the road: once this one sells, I'll send you what it closed for — it directly affects your value. Owners nearby are often surprised. Talk soon!"

Then actually send the sold price. You've just created the most natural seller-lead touch in real estate. Pair it with the farming playbook to turn one open house into a whole-street presence.

Get the email at the door: the sequence only works if sign-in actually captures contacts. Use a tablet form with required email, frame it as "so I can send you the disclosure and floor plan" (a real benefit), and only email people who gave you their address — with a working unsubscribe. CAN-SPAM isn't optional.
