1 · MLS listing description

When to use: turning raw facts into a polished description.

You are an expert real-estate copywriter. Write an MLS listing description for [property address]. Facts: [beds], [baths], [sqft], [key features], [neighborhood highlights]. Tone: [luxury / cozy / investor]. ~120 words. Lead with the single most compelling feature. Be accurate, do not exaggerate, and avoid any fair-housing-sensitive language. End with a clear call to action.

2 · Three social captions from one listing

When to use: announcing a new listing across platforms.

From this listing: [paste description], write 3 social captions: 1) Instagram (warm, 1-2 emojis, 3 hashtags), 2) Facebook (community-focused), 3) X (punchy, under 200 chars). Keep claims factual. Add a CTA to DM for a showing.

3 · New-lead follow-up email

When to use: within minutes of a new online lead.

Write a short, friendly first-touch email to a new buyer lead named [first name] who inquired about [property/area]. Goal: book a quick call. Warm, no pressure, one clear question at the end. Under 90 words. Sign as [your name], [brokerage].

4 · Neighborhood lifestyle paragraph

When to use: enriching a listing or area page.

Write a 70-word lifestyle paragraph about living in [neighborhood, city]. Mention [parks/transit/dining/schools] only if true. Paint a vivid but accurate picture for a [target buyer]. No demographic or fair-housing-sensitive claims.

5 · Objection reframe (price)

When to use: a seller pushes back on your pricing.

A seller says their home is worth more than my recommended list price of [price]. Draft a calm, data-grounded reply that validates their feelings, references [comps/days-on-market/condition], and proposes a next step. Professional, ~120 words.
